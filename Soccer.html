Soccer


Soccer (or Football) is a team sport played between two teams of 11 players on a rectangular field between two players. It is considered as the most popular sport with more than 3 billion fans, 200 million players in over 200 countries. The main objective of the game is to score by moving a ball through the field to opposing team's goal line without touching it with hands or arms, except for goalkeepers in the penalty area and for players performing 'Throw-in'. Players of each team except goalkeeper tries to move the 'football' through the field to opposing team's goal line by methods like dribbling, passing and striking. The team who scored the most goals at the end of the match will be announced as the winner the match. Soccer follows a set of rules known as the Laws of the Game. Matches are overlooked by two referees for any misconducts. If a player breaks the rule, they will be warned or expelled from the match by the referees, indicated by a yellow or red card respectively.

Soccer is governed internationally by International Federation of Association Football aka FIFA. According to FIFA, Soccer originated from ancient China as Cuju (Chinese for Kick Ball), which is similar to modern soccer. FIFA organizes world cups for men and women, once every 4 years. Approximately more than 190 national teams compete in these tournaments to be qualified for the finals, which involves 32 qualified teams competing over a 4-week period. It is the most viewed and followed sporting event in the world, beating Olympic Games. Soccer is the national sport of 5 countries, including France, Haiti, Israel, Italy and Mauritius.
